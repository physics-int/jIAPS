<p>The International Conference of Physics Students (ICPS) is organised every year by a member society of IAPS.</p>
<p>The <strong>Hungarian Association of Physics Students</strong> (Mafihe, NC Hungary) would be pleased to host ICPS <strong>2016</strong>. Since the first conference was organized in Hungary in 1986 by the Mafihe, thus we would love to celebrate its 30th birthday at its birthplace: <strong>Budapest</strong>.</p>
<p>Since 1986 the number of the participants have increased continuously, now we would like to welcome <strong>450 students</strong> from all around the world and involve about 50 people from Hungary as organizers.</p>
<p><strong>Organizers</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://jiaps.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/mafihelogo4.png"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-720" src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/mafihelogo4-300x132.png" alt="mafihe" width="300" height="132" /></a></p>
<p>Hungarian Association of Physics Students (Mafihe) was founded by 137 people, of whom some were also among the founders of IAPS. It has 4 local committees in 3 cities (two in Budapest, one in Debrecen and one in Szeged)</p>
<p><a href="http://jiaps.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSCN0646.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-715" src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/DSCN0646-224x300.jpg" alt="DSCN0646" width="224" height="300" /></a></p>
<p>We organise <strong>scientific</strong> and <strong>cultural</strong> programs for physicists or those who have any contact to physics (not just for Hungarians: check out for example the <a href="http://ortvay.elte.hu/main.html" target="_blank">Ortvay Competition</a> or the <a href="http://bss.mafihe.hu" target="_blank">Balaton Summer School</a>)</p>
<p><em><strong>ICPS organizers</strong></em></p>
<p>The members of the Mafihe are very enthusiastic about organizing the ICPS'16. The organizers would be divided into <strong>committees</strong>, which would have their own tasks. We would like to set up an Organizing Committee, a Scientific Committee and a Steering Committee. There would be individual teams (with a team leader) who are responsible for Excursions, Social Events, PR, Design and Scientific Program in these Committees. There would be an <strong>ICPS Coordinator</strong>, who organize the work of the committees and would have a good grip on the whole situation. The candidate for this position is Tamás Álmos Vámi, the current President of the Mafihe (email: elnok@mafihe.hu).</p>
<p><strong>Date </strong><br />
Beginning: Sunday, 7 August 2016.<br />
End: Sunday, 14 August 2016<br />
<strong>Location</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://jiaps.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/hungaryloc.png"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-718" src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/hungaryloc-300x252.png" alt="hungaryloc" width="300" height="252" /></a></p>
<p>Hungary is a country located in <strong>Central Europe</strong>. It is part of the European Union since 2004 and Schengen since 2007. Our country is famous for the <strong>palinka</strong>, the wines of Tokaj, the <strong>Lake Balaton</strong>, the spicy foods, it’s scenic cities, <strong>The Martians</strong>, it’s rich folk culture, Puskás, the <strong>Rubik Cube</strong>, the Gömböc, Liszt Ferenc, the water polo team and <strong>beautiful girls</strong>. Hungary is one of the five places in the world where the last names precede the first names. The country itself has an intricate history, which starts with the Kingdom of Hungary founded on the Christmas Eve of 1000 A.D. with the coronation of St. Stephen the First.</p>
<p><a href="http://jiaps.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/45.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-711" src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/45-300x200.jpg" alt="45" width="300" height="200" /></a></p>
<p>Budapest is the capital of Hungary. The city has about two million residents, which is the fifth of the population of the country. It is composed of two historical cities: Pest and Buda on the two sides of the Danube. Budapest is often referred as the \textbf{''Queen of Danube''}, which is crowned by eight bridges, and three islands between the two sides. We are proud of the fact that the Buda Castle Quarter, the Banks of the Danube, and the Andrássy Avenue are world heritage sites and the mountains of Buda house many worldwide famous thermal baths.</p>
<p><a href="http://jiaps.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/ELTE.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-716" src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/ELTE-300x199.jpg" alt="ELTE" width="300" height="199" /></a></p>
<p>According to the primary plans the ICPS would take place at the <strong>Eötvös Loránd University (ELTE)</strong>. The University was founded in 1635, making it the oldest Hungarian university. ELTE has more than 30000 students (of which 600 are physicist) studying on eight faculties, making the university also the biggest one in Hungary. The university is not only historical, but internationally acknowledged as well.</p>
<p><a href="http://jiaps.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/sch1.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-723" src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/sch1-200x300.jpg" alt="sch1" width="200" height="300" /></a></p>

<p><strong>Scientific Program</strong></p>
<p><em><strong>Plenary lectures</strong></em></p>
<p>We would like to invite Nobel Laureates. One of our first candidate is Professor Higgs and we have already made some progress with getting contact with him. Although he has no email address, we managed to get his postal address. We will be able to reach Professor Glashow (<em>Nobel Prize in 1979 for his contribution of the electroweak theory</em>), Professor Oláh (<em>Nobel Prize in 1979 for his contribution of carbocation chemistry</em>), Professor Kroto (<em>Nobel Prize in 1996 for fullerens</em>), Professor Gross (<em>Nobel Prize in 2004 for his contribution of the strong interaction</em>) and Professor Geim (<em>Nobel Prize in 2010 for graphene &amp; Ig Nobel Prize for levitating frogs</em>).</p>
<p>Some of our worldwide known scientists are also considered, such as <a href="http://www.rmki.kfki.hu/~diosi/" target="_blank">Professor Diósi</a>, who is known among other things for the Diósi-Penrose theory, <a href="https://hal.elte.hu/~vicsek/" target="_blank">Professor Vicsek</a> who is working on collective motion and complex networks, <a href="http://www.cs.elte.hu/~lovasz/" target="_blank">Professor Lovász</a> who is a well-know matematician (he is the current President of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences) and <a href="https://www.renyi.hu/~szemered/" target="_blank">Professor Szemerédi</a>, who received the Abel Prize in 2012. It would be also nice to invite someone from the Founders, that is why we considers Professor Lévai to ask.</p>
<p><em><strong>Lab tours</strong></em></p>
<p>We plan to organize Lab tours at the Eötvös Loránd University, at the Wigner Research Centre for Physics, at the Kármán Wind Tunnel Laboratory and many more places.</p>

<p><strong>Plan B for the venue - Surprise plan</strong></p>
<p>Our "Surprise plan" would be to place the Conference at Csillebérc, at Wigner Research Centre for Physics. In that case we would get the Lecture Halls for free, the meals would be cheaper and be held at the same building. According to the plans this building will be declared open in 2015, therefore the ICPS would be one of the first big conferences there. Actually, the conference will take place here with good chances. But Plan A for the venue works for sure as a backup plan.</p>
